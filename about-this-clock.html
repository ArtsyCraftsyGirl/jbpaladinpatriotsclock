HOW THE CLOCK WORKS

Our clock features a unique hour marker system with 12 white stars in place of traditional numbers. Each white star represents an hour and lights up in dark blue to show the current hour. At 3:00, for instance, the 3rd star illuminates in dark blue, and the 12th star changes to light blue. To represent minutes, we’ve included the red stripes of the American flag between the hour stars, with each stripe marking one minute of time.

So, at 3:01, the 3rd star remains dark blue, the 12th star continues to be light blue, and one red stripe lights up next to it. By 3:05, the 3rd star continues to stay dark blue, the appropriate number of red stripes are lit up, and the 1st star lights up in light blue, indicating five minutes past the hour. Next to the hour stars, the red stripes will continue to light up until all are lit at 59 minutes past the hour; then, at the strike of a new hour, all red stripes will disappear, and the cycle repeats again.

In the center of the clock, we feature a beautiful ring-shaped circle representative of the original circle of stars on the very first American flag. This circle serves as the seconds counter and it gradually fills with a blue-colored light. We chose the color blue as that was the background color of the circle of 13 stars, just as it is still for the current 50 stars. A choice can be made between a smooth sweeping motion or a Swiss movement for the seconds indicator, and as it completes a 60-second cycle, it then resets itself and repeats the action for the next 60 seconds.
